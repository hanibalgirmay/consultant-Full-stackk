The tailor-made training services should be designed, developed and delivered based on the needs and request of the clients. The enterprise undertakes appropriate and timely OCA, TNA and performance gap assessment focusing on the requested assignment or the enterprise might provide such services based on the ToR or OCA & TNA of the clients. See the Annexed Part Section Three “E” for TNA Form to be applied.

In delivering the Training tasks, the workload shall be calculated as follows

A trainer shall have not more than a six-hour training per day excluding the break time (preferably with Co-trainer/s); this shall be subject to the agreement with the clients A trainer shall not deliver a training more than five days per a week; this shall be subject to the agreement with the clients A trainer’s maximum hour for a training delivery in a week is 30 hours i.e. 6 hours per a day and 5day in a week, this shall be subject to the agreement with the clients; taking into consideration the nature of training and the trainees’ composition & skills

Pre and Post Training Assessment

The focus of the pre and post training assessment is to explore the level of knowledge, skills and experiences of participants on the concepts, principles and contents of the training topic by comparing result of the assessment before and after the actual training. The result of the pre-training assessment shall be the baseline against which the effectiveness of the training shall be measured after its delivery. The same assessment questions shall be given as post-training assessment at the end of the training. The familiarity and prior knowledge/skills can be measured in multiple questions with choices of ranges of excellent, very good, good, fairly, poorly or not at all. To Seethe a Pre-Training Checklist click here.

The Pre-training assessment could be sent to participants via email ahead of the training and this shall help to understand the status of the trainees in terms of their educational background, experience, technical know-how, skills and knowledge. The same assessment should be given at the end of the training (Post Training Assessment) \ to check how much the training has made a difference in the knowledge and skills of participants.

At Post-Training stage /Post Training Assessment

Post-training assessment shall be conducted after the completion of the training or at the end of the training delivery in order to evaluate the appropriateness and effectiveness of the entire package of the training.
